Club positioning

Luxury-leaning tone, minimal palette, stronger typography, and quieter motion. This feels more like a premium fitness brand and less like a landing-page template.

Brand direction

Community, coaching, and wellness — presented like a club, not a coupon gym.

The Lynx reference leans into aspiration. So this version moves Sculpt'd toward a more polished club identity: cleaner hierarchy, less visual noise, stronger copy blocks, and a sharper premium signal.

“Forward-thinking, premium, community-first” is the lane here. The site should feel expensive before the user even checks a package.

What changes

More boutique. More editorial. Still built to rank.

I kept the SEO structure but changed the art direction to feel upscale, confident, and intentionally sparse — closer to the site you pointed at.

Cleaner visual hierarchy

Big serif-led statements, restrained body copy, and better spacing make the brand feel more expensive.

Subtle motion

Scroll reveal instead of loud 3D gimmicks. Motion supports the atmosphere instead of trying to be the atmosphere.

Premium service framing

Offers are positioned like coaching pathways and membership experiences, not price-card widgets.

Offer structure

Use their real offers, but package them with better taste.

  • Small-group training anchored around consistency and community
  • 1:1 personal coaching for clients who want precision and accountability
  • Beginner pathways that remove intimidation from getting started
  • Professional-focused coaching for high-schedule clients
  • Nutrition support and check-ins to keep results moving
